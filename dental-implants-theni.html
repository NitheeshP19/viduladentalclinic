What happens during an implant consultation

An implant consultation is usually centered on evaluating the missing tooth area, surrounding teeth, oral health, and restorative goals. The dentist can explain whether the case may be suitable for implants, what planning is required, and how the final restoration may support appearance and chewing function.

  • Evaluation of the missing tooth area and surrounding oral structures.
  • Discussion of smile goals, chewing needs, and restorative expectations.
  • Review of planning steps before a tooth replacement decision is made.
  • Guidance on how the replacement may fit into long-term dental care.

FAQ

Questions patients ask about dental implants.

Who may consider dental implants?

People with one or more missing teeth often consider implants when they want a stable replacement option that supports chewing, appearance, and confidence.

Why are implants used?

They are used to replace missing teeth and help restore everyday function, smile balance, and long-term comfort when the case is suitable.

Is every patient suitable for implants?

No. Suitability depends on oral health, bone support, gum condition, medical history, and the treatment goals discussed during consultation.

Why is consultation important before implants?

Implants are planning-based treatment. A consultation helps determine whether implants are appropriate and what the restorative pathway may involve.
