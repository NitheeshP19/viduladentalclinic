When to Visit

Common signs that you may need root canal treatment.

Not every toothache needs a root canal, but some symptoms should not be ignored. Early diagnosis helps patients in Theni treat infection sooner and avoid losing a natural tooth.

Persistent tooth pain

Continuous or recurring pain while eating, biting, or even at rest can be a sign that the inner part of the tooth is inflamed or infected and needs careful treatment.

Hot and cold sensitivity that lingers

If sensitivity remains for a long time after drinking something hot or cold, the tooth nerve may be affected beyond what a simple filling can solve.

Swelling, deep decay, or internal damage

Untreated cavities, old restorations, cracks, or infection can spread deeper inside the tooth. A clinical examination helps confirm whether root canal therapy is the right next step.

What to expect during the root canal process

Every case is different, but the usual process includes diagnosis, cleaning of the infected area inside the tooth, shaping and sealing of the root canal space, and then restoring the tooth so it remains strong and usable. In many situations, a crown or final restoration may be advised after the root canal depending on the amount of tooth structure left.

  • Detailed examination to confirm whether the pain is coming from the nerve of the tooth.
  • Removal of infected or damaged tissue from inside the tooth.
  • Careful cleaning and sealing of the root canal space.
  • Restoration planning to help protect the tooth after treatment.

FAQ

Questions patients ask about root canal treatment.

When is root canal treatment recommended?

It is generally recommended when a tooth has deep decay, infection, severe pain, or internal damage that can still be treated while saving the natural tooth.

Is root canal treatment painful?

Modern root canal treatment is intended to relieve pain caused by infection. Careful technique and patient-first treatment planning make the visit far more comfortable than many people expect.

How many visits are needed?

That depends on the tooth, the amount of infection, and the final restoration required. Some cases are straightforward, while others need more than one visit.

Why treat the tooth instead of removing it?

Saving the natural tooth is often the preferred option when possible because it helps maintain chewing, alignment, and long-term function.
